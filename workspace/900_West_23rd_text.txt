EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Adam America Real Estate (“AARE” or the “Sponsor”) is offering an opportunity to invest in a student 
housing development in Austin, Texas. The site is in close proximity the University of Texas at Austin 
(“UT”) – one of the largest and most prestigious institutions in the US. The development comprises of 
982 beds (283 units), approximately 303,995 NSF and 116 parking spaces (the “Project” or “900 West 
23rd Street”). The site is currently an active to-be-demolished student housing building which is 
expected to produce an annual NOI of $1.28 million for August 2024-August 2025. The Project will 
benefit from the current cash flow during the entitlement process.
There is a potential new zoning code which could allow the Sponsor to greatly increase the buildable SF. 
This new zoning code permits development sites to apply the allowable height on any contiguous 
property that is part of the assemblage for the whole development. This increase in density could 
increase the development to north of 1,400 beds and conservatively increase the value of the site by 
$10 million. 
The development has a total Project cost of $160.0 million and is expected to generate an IRR of 
26.25% and an equity multiple of 2.67x for a net profit before tax of $93.6 million. Upon stabilization, 
the Project is expected to generate a yield on cost of 7.37%. Sponsor is underwriting monthly rents of 
$4.69 PSF.
The total purchase price for the deal is $23.2 million. There is currently $13.0 million of debt and fees 
on the property. Of the required equity the seller is leaving $2.8 million in the deal which will be 
subordinate to invested capital. Seller will be taken out upon completion of the approval process.
The Project seeks to serve University of Texas at Austin, one of the top institutions in the US. Currently 
there are only 15 dorms (the newest of which was built in 2007) with 7,809 beds for its 53,082 
students. There is no on-campus living requirement for any student. The Project will be the newest and 
highest quality product in the UT student housing market.
The site is one of the few available within the ‘Inner West Campus’ zoning which allows for high rise 
development. To qualify for a higher density, the Project will be required to comply with The City of 
Austin SMART Housing program. This will require 57 of the Project’s 283 units to be affordable rates.

THE UNIVERSITY OF TEXAS AT AUSTIN
The University of Texas at Austin is a public research university in Austin, Texas and the flagship 
institution of the University of Texas System (“UT System”). With 14 institutions collectively enrolling 
over 256,000 students, the UT System stands as one of the largest public university systems in the 
United States. UT institutions annually produce over 63,000 graduates. The UT System’s $3.8 billion 
research enterprise is one of the nation’s most innovative and ranks second in the nation for federal 
research expenditures.
The University of Texas at Austin offers more than 100 undergraduate and 170 graduate degrees to its 
53,082 students. The main campus has 15 residence halls, the newest of which opened in Spring 2007, 
accounting for 7,809 beds. Academic enrollment greatly exceeds the on-campus housing capacity; as a 
result, most students must live in private residence halls, housing cooperatives, apartments, Greek 
organizations and other off-campus residences. There is no requirement for any student to live on-
campus.
The university houses seven museums and seventeen libraries as well as numerous stadiums and 
entertainment venues. Athletics are a massive draw to the university having won 50 total national 
championships. The 1,439-acre main campus has 150 buildings totaling over 18 million square feet. 
Guadalupe Street on the campus’ Western border features abundant retail with shops, cafes, quick-
bites, and restaurants.
A record 66,109 students applied to attend UT in fall 2023 — a 10% increase from 2022. Of the applied 
students, 9,385 were accepted (up from 2022’s 9,109). Total enrollment rose to 53,082, 1.3% above the 
previous year’s all-time high of 52,384. Additionally, student retention has improved significantly, the 
four-year graduation rate climbed to a record 74.5% (well above the national average of 62.2%), up 
from 52% in 2013.

MARKET OVERVIEW
Austin is the capital of Texas with a population of nearly 1 million (the greater Austin area has over 2 
million). Dubbed the “Live Music Capital of the World,” Austin offers a vibrant culture and is a highly 
sought after place to live. The Austin metro area added 50,105 new residents between July 2022 and 
July 2023, a growth rate of 2.07%.
The city of Austin has approved a $7.1 billion new transportation system comprising multiple train and 
light rail lines, as well as bus routes to enhance accessibility for pedestrians called Project Connect. This 
is planned to dramatically improve accessibility throughout the city.
Austin boasts exceptional outdoor spaces such as Zilker Metropolitan Park, Pease District Park and 
Auditorium Shores at Town Lake. Lady Bird Lake is a river-like reservoir on the Colorado River and is a 
popular recreational area for paddleboards, kayaks, canoes, dragon boats, and rowing shells.
The downtown area of Austin is more than abundant in cuisine, nightlife, cafes, shopping and 
entertainment. The main entertainment strips such as Sixth Street and Red River Cultural District have 
many venues offering regular live music. The world-famous Paramount Theater offers premier 
entertainment options such as concerts and comedy shows. With over 25 museums and many more 
available studios, the art scene remains among the most significant in the US.
Austin’s Silicon Hills is the tech epicenter of Texas with 5,500 startups and tech companies. Many of the 
largest companies in the world have headquarters or regional offices in Austin, including 3M, Amazon, 
Apple, Meta, Google, IBM, Intel, NXP Semiconductors, Oracle, Tesla, Texas Instruments, and Whole 
Foods Market. These companies offer exceptional, high-paying jobs and attract talent worldwide.

RISK FACTORS
17
The real estate industry is cyclical and is sensitive to changes in general economic, interest rates and market conditions nationwide as well as locally – in Austin, TX. Economic conditions could affect interest rates, the extent and timing of the investment activities and the availability of 
investments. Economic conditions could negatively impact the property owner’s ability to carry out its business or cause it to incur losses. The primary elements of risk for this project are summarized below:
Development & Construction: The student housing project is not yet in existence and will be developed and constructed by Sponsor using a general contractor and other third parties. Failure and delays in construction can result in increased cost and/or loss of some or all the 
investment. Additionally, there may be certain entitlements and approvals required for construction by the applicable governmental authorities that cannot be obtained, or the property may have unforeseen environmental issues which result in the potential failure, delay or 
modification of the development and construction of the Project.
Rental Project: The largest component of risk for a student housing project pertains to the prevailing rents at the market following construction and once the units are being leased up. Although the University of Texas at Austin rental market is considered strong, there is no 
assurance that rents used in the business model will be the actual rents following construction in years to come.
Financing: It is contemplated that the construction of the property will be financed in substantial part by utilizing debt which increases the exposure to loss. Interest payments on indebtedness (including mortgages having “balloon” payments) will have to be made regardless of 
the sufficiency of cash flow from the property. The refinancing of the construction loan at the end of the construction period is not guaranteed and subject to market conditions and fluctuations of interest rates that may require an increase in the amount of equity of the 
investment through one or more capital calls.  Investors may be subject to dilution if they fail to participate in capital calls.
Capital Markets: This risk category is corollary to sales risk as the success of the project is sensitive to changes in interest rates. The longer the development takes the higher the chance rates will increase, negatively impacting the cash flow of the property.
Uninsured Losses: Although the Sponsor intends to arrange for customary insurance coverage for the Property, such as comprehensive insurance, including liability, fire and extended coverage, there are certain types of losses (generally of a catastrophic nature, such as wars, 
terrorism, earthquakes and floods) that are either uninsurable or not economically insurable. Should any such uninsured risk occur or cause the destruction or damage of the Property, or should a hazard insured against occur where the loss is in excess of insurance limits or 
should the insurance company be unable to pay the claim, both invested capital and potential profits could be lost.
Lease-Up: Comparable to rental risk, the pace of lease up of units in the property could be impacted materially by changes in market dynamics (e.g. addition of competing projects, unanticipated drop in demand, changes in tax laws, etc.).
No Assurance of Investment Return: There is no assurance that the property owner will be able to generate returns for its investors. Even if the project is successful, there can be no guarantee that the investors will receive distributions in an amount equal to their investment or 
at all.
Illiquidity and Unavailability of Public Market: The investment will be highly illiquid and generally, there will be no readily available market for such investments. Generally, investors will not be able to sell their interests and there will be restrictions on transfer, assignment and 
resale. Moreover, pursuant to the operating agreement, interests are not generally transferable without obtaining the managing member’s prior written consent, and voluntary withdrawal of investors is not allowed, and the interests are not redeemable.
Long-Term Investment: An investment in the property requires a long-term commitment with no certainty of return. Dispositions of such investments may require a lengthy time period. While it is the intention of the Sponsors to achieve target returns over such period, other 
factors such as overall economic conditions, the competitive environment and the availability of potential acquirers may shorten or lengthen holding period.
No Control by Investors: Sponsor has control over the day-to-day operations of the project, however, certain major investors in the project may have certain major decision rights which require its consent.  Investors purchasing interests will have no voice in the day-to-day 
management or other decisions of the company or the property owner’s business.  Accordingly, no person should purchase interests unless he or she is willing to entrust virtually all aspects of management of the property owner and the company to the Sponsor.
Tax Risks: Any investor is subject to a number of significant tax risks. The purchase of interests in the company involves complex federal, state and local tax considerations that differ for each investor.  Each investor will be allocated a portion of the annual income of the company 
without regard to actual cash distributions, thereby possibly resulting in a tax liability that could exceed cash distributions. Additional tax risks may arise from changes in tax legislation which may take place during the life of the Project and which may affect the marketability of 
apartments and/or commercial or may affect the returns earned by investors. Each prospective investor is strongly urged to consult with his or her own tax advisor with respect to his or her particular tax situation, the United States federal, state and local and foreign income tax 
consequences of the purchase, ownership and disposition of interests and condominium units and possible changes in the tax laws or the regulations thereunder.
